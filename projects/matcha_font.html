APS360 Final Project: Matcha Font

In the APS360 course, Applied Fundamentals of Deep Learning, my group developed a font detection AI for our final project. Due to limited computing resources, the training of our model was notably time-consuming. Despite extensive efforts to integrate a pre-trained autoencoder into our system, it consistently underperformed compared to our baseline Convolutional Neural Network (CNN). Ultimately, we enhanced the performance of our model by increasing the size of the CNN and incorporating normalization and dropout layers. This experience underscored the value of simplicity in model design, revealing that more complex models do not always yield better results.

More information about the project can be found in our final report.
