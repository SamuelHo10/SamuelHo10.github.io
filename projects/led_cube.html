LED Cube

(Image Source)

One of my earliest major electronics projects in high school involved designing and assembling an 8x8x8 LED cube. The system was driven by an Arduino Uno and used a 3-to-8 line decoder multiplexer in conjunction with eight D-type octal flip-flops to efficiently control and multiplex the LED matrix. With limited prior experience in electronics, I initially followed a reference schematic sourced online. After verifying the circuit functionality on a breadboard, I migrated the design to EasyEDA, where I created the schematic and PCB layout before ordering custom printed circuit boards. Additionally, to save costs, I opted for copper wires instead of steel, which led to alignment issues due to copper's softness and flexibility, affecting the cube's structure. A notable design issue occurred when I mistakenly ordered an inverting 3-to-8 line decoder multiplexer. I resolved this by adjusting the enable pin logic and modifying the code to produce the correct output. Despite facing issues such as broken LEDs, structural misalignment, and a slight ghosting effect in the display, I successfully completed the project. The cube is fully functional, and I plan to revise the design to improve signal integrity and eliminate display artifacts.
