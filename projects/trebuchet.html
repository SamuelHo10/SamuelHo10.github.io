Physics Class Final Project: Floating Arm Trebuchet

I am very grateful for having a great highschool AP physics teacher who provided us with the opportunity to build a trebuchet for our final project. Instead of constructing a regular trebuchet, my group decided to build a floating arm trebuchet. I tried optimizing the design using python and Lagrangian mechanics based on an online tutorial, but it ended up not working out too well since our idealistic model did not account for friction, air resistance, etc.

Once we settled on a design which satisfied the assignments's size constraints, we got straight to work. In the end, our trebuchet was able to launch a tennis ball well over 20 meters using a counterweight of 10 pounds.
